Bridge Construction

Maymodern Group specializes in various types of bridge construction, leveraging expertise in this essential aspect of civil engineering. Bridges, integral to the built environment, are pivotal structures designed based on load-bearing principles, which vary depending on the type of bridge. Common types include beam, arch, and suspension bridges, each distinguished by its load-bearing structure—whether flat, convex, or concave.

Form Work System

A formwork system involves the use of temporary or permanent molds into which concrete or similar materials are poured during construction. In concrete construction, falsework supports the shuttering molds, facilitating the casting of concrete structures.

Steel Structural Fabrication

Steel structural fabrication encompasses the processes of cutting, bending, and assembling steel to produce a variety of products. This method involves the joining of multiple steel components to construct structures of predetermined sizes and shapes, which are then assembled into buildings, industrial equipment, tools, and various other final products.

Strutting System

Strutting, also known as timbering or shoring, is a technique utilized to offer temporary support to the walls of deep trenches or areas with loose or soft subsoil. This method entails the placement of timber planks and struts along the trench walls, providing crucial temporary reinforcement and stability.

Warehouse Construction

The primary distinction between constructing a warehouse and other types of construction projects lies in their function. A warehouse serves as a facility for storing items belonging to a company. Depending on the requirements of the business, a warehouse may need to incorporate features such as refrigeration, safety measures for storing chemicals, or other specialized facilities. However, at its core, a warehouse is designed to fulfill a straightforward function: to securely store inventory between the stages of manufacturing, transportation, and trading.

Precast Products

Precast concrete is formed by pouring concrete into molds, then curing it off-site before transport and assembly, often using tilt-up techniques. Cast-in-place concrete, on the other hand, is poured directly into on-site forms.

Precast stone resembles natural stone, achieved by incorporating fine aggregates. Technology advancements have introduced expanded polystyrene cores in precast wall panels, enhancing both lightweight construction and energy efficiency in buildings.

Railway Works

Our railway works encompass a comprehensive spectrum of services, including new construction, renewal, repair, and removal of tracks, as well as the installation, renewal, repair, and removal of points.

We also specialize in civil engineering projects in close proximity to railway tracks, such as the renewal and repair of level crossings. Additionally, our expertise extends to track and points inspection, crane tracks installation, and implementation of emergency measures when necessary.

Bridge Demolition Works

Our portfolio encompasses a diverse array of projects, spanning from modest footbridges to sophisticated post-tensioned viaducts situated in bustling urban environments. Our expertise extends across various sectors including rail, highways, and pedestrian infrastructure.

We actively collaborate with demolition contractors, offering comprehensive engineering support tailored to demolition projects. This support encompasses demolition sequencing, structural analysis, and methodological assessments.
